1. Camera Calibration

Camera calibration is the process of estimating the parameters of a camera.

  • Intrinsic parameters: focal length, optical center, skew of the camera.
  • Extrinsic parameters: rotation and translation of the camera with respect to some world coordinate system.

Extrinsic Parameters

Extrinsic parameters of camera is used for projecting the 3D point \((X_w, Y_w, Z_w)\) from the world coordinate to the camera coordinate as \((X_c, Y_c, Z_c)\)

\[\left[\begin{array}{}X_c\\Y_c\\Z_c\end{array}\right]=R\left[\begin{array}{}X_w\\Y_w\\Z_w\end{array}\right] + t\]

Or

\[\left[\begin{array}{}X_c\\Y_c\\Z_c\end{array}\right]=[R|t]\left[\begin{array}{}X_w\\Y_w\\Z_w\\1\end{array}\right]\]

Where \(R\) is 3x3 rotation matrix, \(t\) is 3x1 transtaion matrix, \([R|t]\) is 3x4 combination matrix of \(R\) and \(t\)

Intrinsic Parameters

Intrinsic parameters of camera is used for mapping the 3D point \((X_c, Y_c, Z_c)\) from the camera coordinate onto 2D image plane as \((u, v)\).

\[\left[\begin{array}{}u^{'}\\v^{'}\\w^{'}\end{array}\right] = K\left[\begin{array}{}X_c\\Y_c\\Z_c\end{array}\right]\]

Where \(K = \left[\begin{array}{}f_x&\gamma&c_x\\0&f_y&c_y\\0&0&1\end{array}\right]\) is intrinsic parameter matrix

Then, \(u = \frac{u^{'}}{w^{'}}\) and \(v = \frac{v^{'}}{w^{'}}\)

Finally, from the intrinsic parameters \(K\) and the extrinsic parameters \(R,t\), a 3D point \(M(X_w, Y_w, Z_w)\) in the world coordinate can be projected to a 2D point \(m(u, v, 1)\) in the image plane by

\[Zm = K[R|t]M\]

Where \(Z\) describes associated the depth information of 2D point
