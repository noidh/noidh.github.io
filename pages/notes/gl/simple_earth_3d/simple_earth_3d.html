1. A Simple 3D Earth by QT and OpenGL

A. Calculate Vertices

Actually, the Earth isn't a perfect sphere, but for simple rendering in this article we assume that it's a sphere so that the vertex coordinates can be easily calculated based on the Earth's longitude and latitude. The longitude ranges from -180 to 180 degree and the Prime meridian or zero degree longitude goes through Greenwich. The latitude ranges from -90 degree to 90 degree.

By choosing a step size of 1 degree, there are 360 longitudes and 180 latitudes. We can get better resolution by decreasing the step size, but this results in lower performance during computation and rendering.

Now, from every longitude \(\theta\) and latitude \(\phi\), vertex's coordinate \((x,y,z)\) can be calculated as below.

\(y=r \times sin(\phi)\)

\(xz=r \times cos(\phi)\)

\(x=xz \times sin(\theta)\)

\(z=xz \times cos(\theta)\)

Where, \(r=1.0\)

For rendering the Earth with a color texture, we also need to calculate the texture coordinates. For each vertex, the corresponding texture coordinate is the index of the longitude and latitude normalized from 0 to 1.0.

B. Calculate Indices

From 4 vertices, two triangles can be formed in counterclockwise ordering as below

\(k1 \to k2 \to k1+1\) and \(k1+1 \to k2 \to k2+1\)

C. Vertex Shader

#version 330 core
layout(location = 0) in vec3 aPos;
layout(location = 1) in vec2 aTexCoord;

out VS_OUT
{
    vec2 TexCoord;

} vs_out;

uniform mat4 model;
uniform mat4 view;
uniform mat4 projection;

void main(void)
{
    gl_Position = projection * view * model * vec4(aPos, 1.0);
    vs_out.TexCoord = aTexCoord;
}

D. Fragment Shader

#version 330 core

uniform sampler2D qt_Texture0;

in VS_OUT
{
    vec2 TexCoord;

} fg_in;

out vec4 FragColor;

void main(void)
{
    FragColor = texture(qt_Texture0, fg_in.TexCoord);
}
